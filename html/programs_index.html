Programs

As a federally-qualified health center, Valley Professionals Community Health Center (VPCHC), offers a variety of patient services and programs. The primary purpose of VPCHC is to serve the patients of our local communities, by providing quality, comprehensive health care.

In order to provide the best care possible and to make your visit to VPCHC the most pleasant experience, we encourage you to take a look at the programs and other patient services we offer, that would provide the most benefit for you.

We value the opportunity to serve you and will provide the best care possible!

 

Sliding Fee Scale
If patients are not covered by any type of medical insurance for their medical needs, a sliding fee scale is available for patients to receive a discounted rate. Discounts available are based on patient family size and income in accordance with the most recent U.S. Department of Health & Human Services Federal Poverty Guidelines. Read below, to find out more details and see if you are eligible for sliding fee scale.

Medication Assistance
For those patients who are uninsured or do not have a Medicare Part D prescription drug plan, assistance for many medications may be obtained from the pharmaceutical industry. Patients must fill out required paperwork to begin the Medication Assistance application process, to verify if they are eligible. A medical provider will work with the patient to prescribe generic medications whenever possible for cost savings, but there are cases in which no generic equivalent is available.

Reach Out and Read
Valley Professionals Community Health Center has been fortunate to receive funding to provide age-appropriate books to all children, age six months to 5 years, who are receiving their well-child examination, thanks to the Reach Out and Read organization. Research shows that children who read and are read-to perform better in school and can enjoy a lifelong love of reading and books.
