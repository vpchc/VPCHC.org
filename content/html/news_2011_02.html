New Doctor Joins Local Health Center

September 14, 2011

The Valley Professionals Community Health Center would like to welcome Family Practitioner, Bob Ehresman, M.D.

Dr. Ehresman completed his medical training and graduated from Indiana University School of Medicine in 2008, and completed his undergraduate studies at Indiana State University, graduating in 2004.

Dr. Ehresman completed his residency training through Union Hospital Family Medicine Residency in Terre Haute, and was chief resident during his final year, 2010-2011.

He has resided in the Wabash Valley since the beginning of his residency in 2008, and prior to that as a student at ISU. Dr. Ehresman previously completed electives at the Valley Professionals Community Health Center during his second and third years of residency.

Dr. Ehresman found family medicine most appealing, because “you get to know the patient and their family so well,” as well as the challenges involved in managing such a wide variety of medical issues.

Other clinical interests include chronic disease management, adolescent health, preventative care, newborn care, and prenatal care.

“We are excited to have Dr. Ehresman providing services at our health center. His commitment to the patients, and providing quality patient care will be an invaluable addition to our staff,” stated Elizabeth Burrows, CEO of VPCHC.

Dr. Ehresman is the oldest of three children, and enjoys fishing, music, history, and Chicago Cubs baseball.

Dr. Ehresman will be seeing patients at the VPCHC Clinton office. The health center is open Monday through Friday from 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. If you have any questions, or would like to schedule an appointment, call the office at (765) 832-1003.
